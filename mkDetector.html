This data is taken from a cosmic ray detector test stand located in Atlanta, GA. It is in Rm. 415 of the Science Annex building. All of the graphs show the % variation in cosmic rays detected per hour using a running average. The span of the running average can be adjusted using the slider.

The test stand uses three detectors. The middle detector uses a scintillating liquid sensitive to both muons and neutrons. The graphs shown above display data from this liquid scintillator detector.

The detectors above and below it are sensitive to only muons, and they are used to determine the type of particle we are detecting.
